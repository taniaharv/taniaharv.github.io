Becoming a web developer

If somebody would have told me six months ago that I would be able to make a website, I would not have believed them.

I have been on a 15 week Tech Returners course 'Your Journey Into Tech' culminating in a group project designing a recipe search app.

Since then I have been looking for an app that I could also use on a mobile and/or tablet so I could keep practicing when I didn't have my laptop with me or access to a desktop.

I came across M1m0 and started to refresh my knowledge on what I learnt on the Tech Returners course and to work on a real blog.

Sweetie Gang

Today

Our Foster Mum

My name is Toffee and I was one of the Sweetie Gang. That's me the cheeky one on the right of the picture. There was four of us, two ginger boys - Toffee & Caramel, a ginger girl called Fudge and the odd one out a black boy called Liquorice.

We were found in a paper bale at a recycling depot. No sign of our birth mum.

A man found us and took us home to his wife who did her best to hand-rear us but struggled and eventually contacted a rescue called Rescue Kitties.

Rescue Kitties found us a new mummy called Tania. We were her first hand-rears so she was a little nervous but having fostered a number of litters before, albeit with a cat mummy, she soon got into the swing of bottles, cleaning bums and all the other tasks cat mummies need to do.

Liquorice was the greediest kitten and always grabbed the milk bottles first. Mummy worked out a way of feeding us two at a time and our foster daddy Ahmed also lent a hand.

We grew big and strong with out foster mummies love and care and eventually she found us all lovely homes and furever mummies and daddies.

We can't thank Tania enough for looking after us so well as we would surely have died had someone not found us and got us the care we needed.

I know Tania still thinks of us often and we all still remember our foster mummy, she gave us such a great start in life.
